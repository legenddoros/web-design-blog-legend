US Patent 6506148 B2, 2001

When I think of internet surveliience one of the first things that comes to mind is US Patent 6506148 B2, 2001, titled "Nervous system manipulation by electromagnetic fields from monitors," which describes a method to theoretically influence a person's nervous system through weak, pulsed electromagnetic fields emitted by computer monitors and televisions. I patent doesnt mean that the thing is actualized, and the patent deals with outdated CRT monitors, but I still worry about this. The ability for direct survellience through our phones by the governemnt seems proveable.

Aron Swartz

Aaron Swartz was a brilliant programmer and digital rights activist who fought for free and open internet. He went to Standford briefly in 2004 but dropped out. He co-created RSS in 2000 at 14 years old, helped build Reddit in 2005. He believed knowledge should be accessible to everyone. After attempting to make academic research journals more freely available, by hacking into MIT database, he faced severe federal charges under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act. In 2013, while under prosecution, Swartz sadly ended his own life. This sparked widespread debate about the justice system, digital rights, and the ethics of aggressively prosecuting those who challenge the control of information.

Interesting Court Cases

Carpenter v. United States (2018)

In 2018 it was ruled that ccessing your cell phone data requires a search warrant.

HiQ Labs v. LinkedIn (ongoing developments)

HiQ scraped public LinkedIn profiles to analyze employee data; LinkedIn tried to block it. Ruling: Courts have recognized that data on public platforms may be accessible, but the case raises questions about ownership, consent, and automated data collection. Takeaway: Sets precedent for limits on data scraping versus corporate control of digital information.

I looked at my Instagram and Facebook ad history, and these line up exactly with my search results. Cultural marketing is different than exclusionary matketing. If ad targeting results in excluding protected groups from opportunity, that’s obviously illegal discrimination.
