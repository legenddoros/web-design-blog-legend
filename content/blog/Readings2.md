## Aron Swartz

Aaron Swartz was a brilliant programmer and digital rights activist who fought for free and open internet. He went to Standford briefly in 2004 but dropped out. He co-created RSS in 2000 at 14 years old, helped build Reddit in 2005. He believed knowledge should be accessible to everyone. After attempting to make academic research journals more freely available, by hacking into MIT database, he faced severe federal charges under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act. In 2013, while under prosecution, Swartz sadly ended his own life. This sparked widespread debate about the justice system, digital rights, and the ethics of aggressively prosecuting those who challenge the control of information.

# Interesting Court Cases

## Carpenter v. United States (2018)

In 2018 it was ruled that ccessing your cell phone data requires a search warrant.

## HiQ Labs v. LinkedIn (ongoing developments)

HiQ scraped public LinkedIn profiles to analyze employee data; LinkedIn tried to block it.
Ruling: Courts have recognized that data on public platforms may be accessible, but the case raises questions about ownership, consent, and automated data collection.
Takeaway: Sets precedent for limits on data scraping versus corporate control of digital information.
