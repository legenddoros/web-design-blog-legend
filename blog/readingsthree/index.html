The Web's Grain - Frank Chimero, 2015

The old school audio handshake is something to be exapanded upon musically. I remember it well in the 90s. I actually remember chat rooms in the mid 90s, that was our early internet.

French philosopher Gaston Bachelard: “We begin in admiration and end by organizing our disappointment.”

As long as that disapointment is followed by action! Im curious about this thought that profundity comes from complication. Freedom with vanilla, or clarity. The layout examples in the article examplify the advantage of simple layouts. I compare this in writing as spoon feeding. In writmg you also want to be clear, not over written. We call that “bombastic”. So I appreciate this zen buddhist concept for any art making created for an audience to easily understand. The "bicycle bear websites" demonstrate tricks, but are not always practical.

I also like this concept of edgelessness in the creation of websites, the dynamic field and the thought of creating asssemblages. It made me recall the late Claude Monet paintings where his vision was diminished by cataracts where he created these large scale paintings that look almost abstract and never ending. I also thought of Robert Rauschenbergs assemblage "Canyon" at MOMA, 1958, and the thought about when does a piece of art end, or how its ending alludes to its continuation.

Automation and Convenience as a Trojan horse

I found an interesting interview with Eric at an AI conference in Singapore last year, 2025. Some dangerous elements of AI in the future. Machines encourage averages. This is not something we want to promote. There will be no recourse. No phone number for Google even now. He imagined more and more people are going to be screaming at macines.

ChatGPT is a Blurry JPEG of the Web - Ted Chiang, 2023

This idea of lossless versus lossy.

“If the restored file is identical to the original, then the compression process is described as lossless: no information has been discarded.”

Judging the rooms and thinking of them as similiar caused the digital image to show all rooms the same size. You could also describe the computer as processing in patterns, and this is one of its the pitfalls. For instance, a strange thing happened to me once. I asked ChatGPT to analyze a story and give critique. I did a few of the same query. Once, after the 4th query say, the analysis shockingly actually made up stuff that wasn’t in the story! Events that happened, but still kept the essential gist of the characters. A hallucination as defined in the article. The “common technique used by lossy compression algorithms is interpolation”.

I've used AI to critique my writing, and similiarly when asked for best lines or what to edit, after several repeat queries the same line will sometimes eventually be attributed as both “best” and “needed to edit or problematic “.
